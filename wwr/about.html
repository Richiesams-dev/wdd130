History

White water rafting is an exhilarating outdoor activity that involves navigating through turbulent river rapids in an inflatable raft. It combines adventure, teamwork, and a deep appreciation for nature's power and beauty. Rafting trips can vary in difficulty, from gentle floats on calm rivers to challenging descents through Class V rapids. Participants are typically equipped with safety gear, including helmets and life jackets, and are guided by experienced professionals who ensure a safe and enjoyable experience.

It has a rich history that dates back to ancient times when people used simple rafts to navigate rivers for transportation and fishing. The modern sport of white water rafting began in the 19th century, with the first recorded descent of the Colorado River through the Grand Canyon in 1869 by John Wesley Powell and his crew. The sport has also become a significant part of the outdoor adventure tourism industry, with many companies offering guided rafting trips on some of the most scenic and challenging rivers.
